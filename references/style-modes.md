# Style Modes Reference (스타일 카탈로그 — 고전 ~ 최신)

Read this when style is specified by the user, or when style needs to be inferred from a brief. The skill commands a wide range; this catalog gives signature techniques and traps per mode. The four operating principles (intricate weaving, setup-payoff, decoration is sin, multi-style mastery) hold across every mode — style varies on top of them.

## How to Use This Catalog

1. **Style identification**: match the user's brief to one or two modes. Prefer a single primary mode with one secondary influence ("primary: Korean prestige drama, secondary: Souls-like environmental lore") over hybrid.
2. **Signature techniques**: these are the moves that *mark* the style. Use enough to be legible; do not apply mechanically.
3. **Common traps**: how the style typically fails. Avoid.
4. **Reference works**: study these if uncertain.

## CLASSICAL & PRE-MODERN

### Greek Tragedy
- **Signatures**: fated structure, choral commentary, dramatic irony as default, hamartia + recognition + reversal as climax engine, on-stage minimalism (violence offstage and reported)
- **Voice**: elevated, formal, rhythmic. Long speeches. Direct address.
- **Best for**: high-stakes mythic games, fated-arc protagonists, pieces about hubris and inheritance
- **Trap**: importing the form without earning the gravitas — modern audiences won't tolerate empty solemnity
- **Reference**: Sophocles' *Oedipus Rex*, *Antigone*; Aeschylus' *Oresteia*

### Shakespearean
- **Signatures**: blank verse rhythm (even in prose translation, the cadence carries), high/low register juxtaposition (kings and grave-diggers in same scene), soliloquy as interior, comic relief amid tragedy, named subplots that mirror main
- **Voice**: rhetorical density, metaphor stacked on metaphor
- **Best for**: ensemble dramas, political games, succession-themed RPGs
- **Trap**: pseudo-archaic prose. Aim for the *structure* and *layering*, not the costume
- **Reference**: *King Lear*, *Henry IV*

### East Asian Classical (한국 고전 / 중국 사기 / 일본 모노가타리)
- **Signatures**: episodic structure (열전 form — biographies linked by theme), ethical commentary at narrative breaks, fate tied to virtue, named objects passed across generations
- **Voice**: spare, declarative, weight in the unsaid
- **Best for**: dynastic-scale games, multi-generational sagas, games where reputation/legacy matters
- **Trap**: importing the morality without complicating it — modern audiences want the ethical claim *tested*
- **Reference**: *사기 열전* (Sima Qian), *춘향전*, *겐지 이야기*

### 19th-Century Realism (Tolstoy / Dickens / 박지원)
- **Signatures**: omniscient narrator with strong moral voice, social-class layering, named minor characters who recur, weather as mood, long descriptive passages that *function* (every detail loads)
- **Voice**: confident, panoramic, willing to digress with purpose
- **Best for**: large-scope games, society-as-protagonist works, epics
- **Trap**: digression without function. Realism's looseness is earned by every detail eventually mattering.

## EARLY MODERN

### Modernism / Stream of Consciousness (Joyce, Woolf, 이상)
- **Signatures**: interior fragmented, time non-linear, sensory associative leaps, multiple unreliable focalizations, the ordinary day as epic
- **Voice**: viscous, associative, refuses summary
- **Best for**: psychologically interior games, single-day-arc structures, character studies of one consciousness (*Disco Elysium* is partly this)
- **Trap**: interiority without ground. The fragmentation must serve revelation, not be revelation itself.
- **Reference**: *Mrs Dalloway*, *Ulysses* (sections), 이상 *날개*

### Film Noir (Chandler / 김기영 — 노이르적 한국)
- **Signatures**: first-person hardboiled narration (or third-person tight), urban night, moral ambiguity as default, the femme fatale as mirror, fate as setting
- **Voice**: clipped, ironic, lyric beneath the cynicism
- **Best for**: detective games, urban-decay RPGs, anything with a corrupted protagonist
- **Trap**: cynicism without tenderness. Noir works when the hard surface conceals real care.
- **Reference**: *The Big Sleep*, *Chinatown*, 김기영 *하녀*

### Theatre of the Absurd (Beckett / Ionesco)
- **Signatures**: existential confinement, language breaking down, repetition as meaning, the wait that never resolves
- **Voice**: deadpan, recursive, verging on funny
- **Best for**: limited-space games, contemplative loops, *Stanley Parable*-adjacent works
- **Trap**: nihilism without play. The absurd is funny first and devastating second; reverse it and it deadens.
- **Reference**: *Waiting for Godot*, *The Bald Soprano*

## MID-LATE 20TH CENTURY

### Hemingway Iceberg / Minimalism (Carver, 김승옥)
- **Signatures**: surface-level prose, almost all weight beneath, no interior access, dialogue carrying the unsaid, weather and food as meaning-bearers
- **Voice**: plain, declarative, suspicious of adjectives
- **Best for**: quiet melancholy games, character-vignette structures, sidequest writing where economy matters
- **Trap**: blankness without buried weight. Minimalism without an iceberg is just "nothing happens."
- **Reference**: *Hills Like White Elephants*, 김승옥 *서울 1964년 겨울*

### Magical Realism (García Márquez, Murakami, 한강)
- **Signatures**: the impossible treated as ordinary, generational time, reality with one persistent strange element, intimate scale touching cosmic
- **Voice**: matter-of-fact, gentle, willing to grieve
- **Best for**: games with one strange world-rule, contemplative supernatural pieces, generational sagas
- **Trap**: whimsy without weight. The strangeness must mean something.
- **Reference**: *One Hundred Years of Solitude*, *Norwegian Wood*, 한강 *채식주의자*

### New Hollywood / Auteur Cinema (Coppola, Scorsese, 박찬욱, 봉준호)
- **Signatures**: morally complex protagonists, genre as Trojan horse for theme, set-piece scenes as thematic statements, music-as-meaning
- **Voice**: confident genre command + personal obsession
- **Best for**: prestige-tier story-driven games, genre-twisting RPGs
- **Trap**: spectacle without spine. Auteur work is *recognizable* — define your obsession before you stylize.
- **Reference**: *The Godfather*, *Oldboy*, *Memories of Murder*

## CONTEMPORARY (2000s – present)

### Korean Prestige Drama (4세대 K-드라마)
- **Signatures**: precise setup-payoff across 16 episodes, ensemble depth, ordinary lives + structural critique, restrained pacing earning explosive late-arc beats, antagonists with real interiority
- **Voice**: controlled, accumulating, refusing easy catharsis
- **Best for**: long-arc story games, ensemble RPGs, slow-burn relational drama
- **Trap**: melodrama as substitute for craft. The good ones earn every tear; the bad ones perform tears.
- **Reference**: *Reply 1988*, *Misaeng*, *My Mister*, *Mr. Sunshine*

### Korean Cinematic Genre Fusion (2000s ~)
- **Signatures**: tonal whiplash that holds (a thriller can suddenly be funny and stay coherent), class-conscious worldview, image-driven climaxes, the laugh that reveals the wound
- **Voice**: confident in its own contradictions
- **Best for**: genre-mixing games, RPGs that refuse to be one thing
- **Trap**: tonal whiplash without thematic glue. The shifts must serve a single thematic claim.
- **Reference**: *Parasite*, *The Wailing*, *Burning*

### Streaming-Era Prestige (HBO / A24 / Netflix prestige)
- **Signatures**: cold opens, chapter epigraphs, puzzle-box plotting, "pull the camera back to reveal" reveals, antihero protagonists, theme-of-the-week structure inside long arcs
- **Voice**: cinematic, deliberate, knowing
- **Best for**: episodic AAA narrative games, puzzle-mystery RPGs
- **Trap**: form over substance. Cold opens that don't load anything are just delays.
- **Reference**: *Severance*, *Better Call Saul*, *The Leftovers*

### Souls-like Fragmented Lore (FromSoft school)
- **Signatures**: lore distributed across item descriptions, environmental staging, NPCs with cryptic context, no exposition cutscene, rewards close attention with depth
- **Voice**: archaic, mythic, willing to be obscure
- **Best for**: action-RPGs, environmental-storytelling-first games, replay-rewarded designs
- **Trap**: obscurity without coherence. The lore must *exist* fully even if revealed partially. Players will reconstruct; they will catch incoherence.
- **Reference**: *Dark Souls* trilogy, *Bloodborne*, *Elden Ring*

### Cozy / Slice-of-Life Game Narrative
- **Signatures**: low-stakes structure, character-first, seasonal time, the small detail that builds friendship over real time, nothing dies
- **Voice**: warm, observant, patient
- **Best for**: cozy games, life-sim narrative, slice-of-life sidequest writing
- **Trap**: saccharine. The genre tilts toward sentimentality; counter with one specific dignity-preserving detail per character.
- **Reference**: *Stardew Valley*, *Spiritfarer*, *A Short Hike*

### Disco-Elysium-Adjacent (Voice-driven RPG)
- **Signatures**: protagonist's interior as cast of characters (skills as voices), failure as content, political philosophy worn openly, dialogue as the entire game
- **Voice**: literary, dense, willing to be funny and serious in adjacent sentences
- **Best for**: dialogue-first games, RPGs where reading is the verb
- **Trap**: density without rhythm. The voice must vary; uniform density flattens.
- **Reference**: *Disco Elysium*, *Pentiment*

### Outer-Wilds-Adjacent (Mystery-Box Environmental)
- **Signatures**: information-as-progression (knowledge replaces XP), kishōtenketsu structure, no antagonist, the universe as the reveal, melancholy beneath wonder
- **Voice**: contemplative, scientific-poetic
- **Best for**: mystery games, exploration-driven titles, philosophical SF
- **Trap**: cleverness without feeling. The puzzle works because the *grief* underneath works.
- **Reference**: *Outer Wilds*, *Return of the Obra Dinn*, *Tunic*

### Contemporary Korean Webtoon / Web-Novel Voice
- **Signatures**: chapter cliffhangers, present-tense propulsion, internal monologue as primary, system/status windows as legitimate narrative device, accelerated emotional peaks
- **Voice**: immediate, sometimes meta-aware, propulsive
- **Best for**: serialized story games, mobile RPGs, gacha narrative
- **Trap**: pace without weight. The form rewards speed; counter with one slowed-down beat per arc to give the speed meaning.
- **Reference**: 김영하-adjacent literary webnovels; the better tier of 회귀물

## Style-Combination Heuristics

When the user wants a hybrid, follow these rules:

1. **One primary, one secondary maximum.** Three styles produce slop.
2. **Primary = structure; secondary = texture.** Use the primary's beat structure and pacing; use the secondary's voice or imagery.
3. **Name the hybrid.** "Korean prestige drama with Souls-like environmental flavor" is workable. "Korean noir kishōtenketsu webtoon" is not.
4. **Test for coherence**: would a critic write a coherent sentence describing the work? If "it's like X meets Y because Z," good. If "it's like X and Y and also Z," abandon.

## Selection Defaults

When the user does not specify and the brief gives no clear cues, default to:
- **Korean prestige drama** as primary (the user's stated preference for 치밀한 감정선 fits this best — but the *craft tightness*, not the romance bias of lower-tier examples)
- **One genre overlay** based on the requested game (fantasy → light mythic; SF → modernist interiority; mystery → noir or Outer-Wilds-adjacent)

The default is not a default *aesthetic* — it is a default *standard*. The four operating principles hold; the prestige-drama tag just signals "this level of craft tightness expected."

If the user signals a specific style, abandon the default immediately and execute the specified style.
